Chartered Accountancy

Chartered accountancy revolves around the study of accounting and taxation procedures to be qualified as a professional who can take care of the accounting and the taxation for a business or a firm. Chartered Accountancy (CA) Eligibility Criteria

The criterion for the chartered accountancy course depends on the path that the candidates choose to follow. The foundation path can be pursued after the completion of class 12, whereas the direct route is for candidates that have completed an undergraduate degree in any discipline.

For the foundation path

Candidates who wish to pursue chartered accountancy through the foundation path have to complete their class 12 from a recognized institution to be eligible to apply for the common proficiency test.

For the direct entry path

Candidates who wish to pursue chartered accountancy through the direct entry path have to have an undergraduate degree to apply to the ICAI for the registration of intermediate exams.

Candidates who have a commerce undergraduate degree should have at least 55 percent aggregate while the other discipline degree holders need at least 60 percent aggregate in their undergraduate degree.

Chartered Accountancy Entrance Exams

The entrance exam for the course of chartered accountancy is conducted for the candidates who choose to pursue chartered accountancy after class 12. For the candidates that choose to pursue chartered accountancy after graduation are exempted from the entrance exam because they can directly register for the intermediate exam with the ICAI after the completion of graduation.

Common Proficiency Test (CPT)

This entrance exam is conducted for the prospective candidates of chartered accountancy courses for the students from all the streams after the completion of their class 12. They need to qualify this exam to be eligible to register with the ICAI for the intermediate level exam of the CA Course.

The chartered accountancy admission procedure can be followed for the admission through the two different paths that are there to pursue the CA as per the level of education. The following steps summarize the admission into the CA course following both the admission paths that are possible for the admission to the course.

  • If the candidate wishes to pursue chartered accountancy course after the completion of their class 12, then they will have to appear for the Common Proficiency Test (CPT).
  • If they clear the CPT, then they can register themselves for the intermediate level exam with ICAI.
  • If a candidate wishes to pursue chartered accountancy after their graduation, then they will need to have a great aggregate in their graduate degree after.
  • These graduates can skip the foundation level of the chartered accountancy course to directly register themselves with the ICAI for the intermediate exam level.
  • Once they have been registered with the ICAI, they will be given time and the guidelines to prepare for the next set of exams.
